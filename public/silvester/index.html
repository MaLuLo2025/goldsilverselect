1. Price Data Sources

Real-time precious metals prices displayed on this platform are provided by a licensed, subscription-based market data service that aggregates spot pricing from major global precious metals exchanges and over-the-counter (OTC) markets. These prices reflect indicative spot market values and may differ from prices available through any particular dealer, exchange, or transaction venue.

Historical precious metals prices used in backtesting and analysis are sourced from the London Bullion Market Association (LBMA) PM Fix. The LBMA Gold Price and LBMA Silver Price are benchmark prices determined through an electronic auction process administered by ICE Benchmark Administration (IBA). The LBMA PM Fix is the most widely used benchmark for gold and silver transactions globally, referenced by central banks, mining companies, and institutional investors. The gold auction occurs twice daily (10:30 AM and 3:00 PM London time); the silver auction occurs once daily (12:00 noon London time). Historical LBMA prices are available at prices.lbma.org.uk.

Prices may vary between sources due to differences in timing, settlement conventions, and geographic markets. London and New York prices, for example, may diverge during periods of market stress or illiquidity. Users should not rely on any price displayed here as the basis for an executable trade without independent verification.

These sources are widely regarded as authoritative and are relied upon by institutional investors, economists, and policymakers worldwide.

Specific data series used include, but are not limited to: the 10-Year Treasury Inflation-Indexed Security yield (real interest rates), the Trade-Weighted U.S. Dollar Index, the ICE BofA U.S. High Yield Option-Adjusted Spread, the Consumer Price Index, and manufacturing activity indicators. Market volatility data is sourced from the CBOE Volatility Index (VIX). Copper price data is sourced from commodity exchange futures.

While these sources are believed to be accurate and reliable, SILVESTER makes no guarantee regarding the accuracy, completeness, or timeliness of any third-party data. Economic data is subject to revision by the issuing agencies.
